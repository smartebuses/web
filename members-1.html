Project Coordinator

Dr. Alejandro Arbelaez received his PhD in computer science from University of Paris XI for his work on applying learning-based techniques to solve combinatorial problems. After finishing his PhD, he worked as a post-doctoral researcher at the University of Tokyo (2011-13) and University College Cork (2013-16). He is currently a Lecturer in the School of XXX at University College Cork. Dr. Arbelaez is the author of more than 40 research papers in prestigious conferences, journals, international workshops, and 2 two chapters in prestigious books in the field of artificial intelligence.

DBLP

Dr. Laura Climent is a Lecturer in the Computer Science department at University College Cork. Before she was a postdoctoral researcher in the Insight Centre for Data Analytics in the University College Cork in Ireland. She obtained her Ph.D. in Computer Science from the Universitat Politècnica de València. Dr. Climent’s research interests are within the area of Combinatorial Optimisation and Decision Analytics, including among others sustainability problems and problems with uncertainty. Specifically, she is interested in:Constraint Programming, Mixed Integer Linear Programming and Metaheuristics. Also, their combination with Data Mining and Machine Learning techniques.

DBLP

Dr. Ignacio Castineiras graduate with a PhD in Computer Science from Complutense University of Madrid in 2014 for his work on multi-paradigm declarative programming systems. After completing his PhD he worked as a postdoctoral researcher at University College Cork (2014–2015). The main goal of the postdoc was to develop a scalable decision support tool to optimise the workload allocation of single and distributed Data Centers for the EU project GENiC (Globally optimised Energy Efficiency Data Centers). Since September 2015, he works as a Lecturer at the Computer Science Department in CIT.
